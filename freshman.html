Pinterest05

As the baby freshman I was, the thoughts of post-grad and a full-time career were practically non-existent. I didn't know what I wanted to study nor what I wanted to do in the future.

It wasn't until my friend who worked at Pinterest invited me to his office. Food bars, stylish and modern furniture, comfortable work spaces, people constantly interacting with one another, and a beautiful view at the rooftop. Such simple thing now that I'm looking back, but is all that it took for me to become determined to work at place like there and eventually kick-start my professional journey as a little freshman.

Meet the Pam04

The first group in Berkeley I've felt really close to.

When I joined my pam (a big/little type of program), I came to met amazing people whom I all viewed as a role model in multiple ways.

From driving me out to their favorite food places outside Berkeley, to bringing me food when I was sick. From teaching me how to apply for EBT to housing me when I was kicked out of the dorms once May hit. From being some of my first friends in Berkeley to friends I hope to have for the rest of my life. Much love.

Dorm Life03

Lights off, headset on—times in Freeborn 811.

With a TV under the loft, RGB lightbulb for the ambience, a view of the sun setting from the topmost floor facing the hills, and two semi-cool roommates (just kidding, love you guys), my time at the dorm was always laid-back fun. Studying, listening to music, watching weekly shows together, getting an occasional #1 Victory Royale—just a blast of nostalgia.

Avalon Club02

I met this group of people at a club's weekly game nights. And each time we went we only played this one game: Avalon. Originally playing from 8-10pm, we'd eventually start playing outside game nights, having countless hours playing until 4am, alongside random talks, and eventually becoming really good friends.

Senior Weekend01

As a senior in high school, I faced a huge amount of insecurity in going to college, especially being the first in my family to go to a 4-year university. Cost, academic rigor, moving away from home, being somewhere new. Then came an invitation from Berekeley, offering a weekend visit the campus and see what it was like. During that weekend I SIR'ed.

Fast forward a year later, I had an opportunity to give people who were in the exact same shoes as me the year before that same experience. Showing them the ropes, what life was like, and ultimately helping them give the confidence that they could succeed in UC Berkeley.
